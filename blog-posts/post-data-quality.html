Why data quality matters in maps

Maps are only as good as the data behind them. In our MSc work we use a simple quality checklist before any analysis or dashboard.

  • Completeness – are features missing or duplicated?
  • Accuracy – do coordinates, lengths and attributes make sense?
  • Consistency – same names, codes and units everywhere.
  • Timeliness – when was the dataset last updated?

Quick wins we apply: fix common typos, snap obvious geometry errors, reproject to the right CRS, and add a simple data dictionary so the next person understands every field.

News: many organizations are releasing open QA rules for GIS. It’s a great time to build lightweight validation into your workflow.
